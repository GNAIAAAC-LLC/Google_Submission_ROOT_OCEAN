Governance Purpose

SSGPT6 governance exists to keep the platform organized, transparent, reviewable, and aligned with its stated mission. It is designed to help people understand what the platform does, what it does not do, how decisions are tracked, and how services, projects, and communication pathways are managed responsibly.

Accountability

Important decisions should be visible, documented, and reviewable.

Human Oversight

Automation supports people, but final direction remains human-controlled.

Clarity

Customers and partners should understand where to go, what to expect, and how the platform works.

Core Governance Layers

The SSGPT6 governance structure can be understood as a set of working layers.

1. Public Information Layer

  • homepage
  • about page
  • services page
  • projects page

2. Customer Access Layer

  • pricing and memberships
  • contact pathways
  • support center
  • billing communication

3. Reporting Layer

  • structured summaries
  • submission drafts
  • application tracking
  • recordkeeping

4. Review Layer

  • claim review
  • language safety
  • page updates
  • content readiness

5. Automation Support Layer

  • draft generation
  • voice support
  • submission preparation
  • workflow assistance

6. Founder Direction Layer

  • mission setting
  • approval
  • strategic direction
  • project prioritization

Decision Rules

Rule 1: The platform should not promise features that are not implemented.

Rule 2: Payment, support, and access pathways should always lead somewhere real.

Rule 3: Public claims should be clear, supportable, and reviewable.

Rule 4: Automation should assist preparation, tracking, and organization—not silently impersonate outside submission or approval.

Rule 5: Accessibility should remain a visible part of the system for seniors and disabled users.

Governance note: The best way to build trust is to show structured systems, real pages, accurate language, clear permissions, and visible support routes.
